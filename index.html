About

Typically food contributes to 21% of annual carbon emissions of an average American. This total includes emissions from growing, processing, distributing, selling, and cooking food, as well as additional emissions from disposing of any leftover waste products.


When these are added together, the carbon “foodprint” of an average American sums to over 5000 kg of CO2 emissions per year. And if we consider that a person eats 3 meals a day, each meal would contribute about 5 kg of Co2 emissions.



The JHFood Solution

When you sit down for a meal, you typically don’t reflect on the environmental consequences of the meal, especially in an all-you-can-eat dining hall.


JHFood calculates the total carbon dioxide equivalent value of your meal based on the dish(es) of choice, serving size, and composition of each dish. The Johns Hopkins University dining halls offer a wide range of options, from steamed cauliflower to vegan chorizo burritos. Given the countless vegetarian, vegan, and humane meat options offered, the JHFood CO2 calculator helps assess the eco-friendliness of your menu choices.


--- Check out our calculator! ---

The JHFood website also offers school-wide challenges that promote more green eating habits. You are encouraged to commit (share) your meal choices and participate in meatless monday, go vegan for a week, or compete with your friends for the lowest CO2 score.
